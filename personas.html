Devotion Venture Studio accepts three different founder personas for our cohorts

Pick the lane that fits the person you will refer.

Growth hacker Mgmt Consultant Technical Builder

Growth hacker who can sell from scratch

  • A commercially driven person who is good at sales and understanding the customer. Has a growth journey of 0 to 20 MSEK ARR or similar in their background.
  • Driven by GPQ generates profits quotient . Eager to grow revenue and loves to optimize income by experimenting.
  • Can build the first sales machine. Playbook, funnels, SDR team.
  • Wants to be CEO slash founder and receive operational support marketing, legal, finance from day one.

The Mgmt Consultant who wants to build for real

  • A person who is or recently was at McKinsey, BCG, Bain, or similar business development roles. Had enough of delivering PowerPoints for others.
  • Shows tenacity and GSDQ Get Shit Done quotient . Successfully driven projects despite poor conditions.
  • Wants to own their own P and L, not just analyze others.
  • Wants to be CEO slash founder in a new company with product, customers, and team in place.

The Technical Builder who can code V1

  • A senior developer or technical product person who has always been the engine in product building but never received the ownership they deserve.
  • Has built V1 versions before, internally or as side projects, and can translate ideas into products in weeks, not months.
  • Has a high FuckQ unshakeable conviction quotient about their own capacity to build solutions that others say are impossible.
  • Wants to be CEO slash founder and receive commercial support marketing, sales, growth from the start.

What we don't look for

  • The Idea Collector. Talks about ten business ideas but has never built anything all the way. Lacks focus and perseverance.
  • The Security Seeker. Wants a stable job and to avoid risk. Prefers to minimize uncertainty rather than chase growth.
  • The Theoretician. Gets stuck in strategy, models, and analyses instead of quickly shipping MVP, customers, and revenue.
